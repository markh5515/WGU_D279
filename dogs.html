Top Pet Care Tips for Dogs:

  • Ensure your dog receives the necessary vaccinations.
  • Regular grooming is essential for your dog's health. This includes frequent brushing, checking for insects, bathing, and monitoring for allergic reactions to cleaning products.
  • As dogs age, they require fewer meals. Hydration is crucial, as dehydration can lead to serious health issues.
  • Provide your dog with 30-120 minutes of exercise or brisk activity daily.

Diet and Exercise

As dogs mature, they should be fed fewer meals. Puppies should be nursed for the first two months, then introduced to three meals per day from three to six months old. Reduce to two meals per day from six months to one year old, and finally to one meal per day after one year old.

A dog's body is composed of 60-70% water. According to the ASPCA, a 10% decrease in body water can cause illness, and a 15% loss can be fatal. Ensure your dog stays hydrated!

Dogs need regular exercise to maintain their health. The recommended exercise time varies by breed and size, but generally ranges from 30 to 120 minutes per day. Brisk walks are sufficient, and a variety of toys can encourage physical activity. Ensure toys are age-appropriate and do not pose a choking hazard.

Grooming and Handling

Keeping your dog clean is essential for its health. Brush frequently to reduce shedding and prevent matted and tangled fur, which can be difficult to remove. Check for insects such as ticks and fleas, especially after visiting wooded areas.

Bathing is important for your dog's health and can be a workout depending on the dog's temperament. Rinse all the soap out thoroughly, as any residual soap can cause a rash. Use soap formulated for dogs to avoid allergic reactions.

Handle your dog with care. For small dogs, cradle them with one hand under the chest and the forearm supporting the back half. For large dogs, reach under the belly with both arms perpendicular to the dog, supporting the chest and rear as you lift.




Vaccinations and Medications

You should always consult with your veterinarian, but there is a core set of vaccines that are typically recommended. These include vaccines for hepatitis, rabies, and parvovirus. Additional vaccines may be recommended by your vet based on your dog's environment. Some of these include Bordetella bronchiseptica, Borrelia burgdorferi, and Leptospira bacteria.
