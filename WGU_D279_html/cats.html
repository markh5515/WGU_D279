Top Pet Care Tips for Cats:

  • 0 to 4-Weeks: Monitor the kitten's growth carefully while it is nursing.
  • Age 4-Weeks to 1-Year: Introduce a combination of soft and hard foods. Consult your vet for dietary recommendations.
  • Age 1-Year to 7-Years: Reduce food intake as growth slows. Provide two meals a day to prevent overeating.
  • Age 7-Years and More: Older cats need less fat and calories. Look for high-quality protein in their food.
Age 0 to 4-Weeks

It's crucial for the queen (a term for a female cat that is pregnant or nursing) to nurse her young. Monitor the kitten’s growth to ensure it is progressing steadily. If a kitten is not growing sufficiently, a caretaker may need to feed it directly with a bottle or feeding tube.

Reasons for inadequate weight gain in kittens include:

  • Too many siblings competing for milk
  • Gastrointestinal disease
  • Extreme environmental conditions or unsanitary conditions
Age 4-Weeks to 1-Year

Kittens can start eating soft wet food around three to four weeks after birth. According to the ASPCA, kittens should eat half to one cup of dry food or six to nine ounces of wet food per day. If your kitten struggles with hard food, add a small amount of water to soften it. Consult your veterinarian for dietary recommendations based on your kitten's breed and needs.

Age 1-Year to 7-Years

As cats mature, their activity levels and metabolism decrease. Avoid leaving food out all day. Instead, provide meals twice a day to prevent obesity and other weight-related issues.

Age 7-Years and More

As cats age, their bodies undergo many changes. They require less fat and calories but more high-quality proteins. When buying cat food, look for specific proteins like "salmon" rather than generic terms like "fish" to ensure higher quality.
